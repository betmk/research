Sparta Framework: 6-Part Thesis

1. Demand destruction is the only fix

~10M bbl/d lost through Hormuz closure, only 3–4M offset by SPR releases + voluntary production reduction, leaving a 6–7M bbl/d unresolved gap. OPEC spare capacity of 3.5M bbl/d is insufficient to bridge the deficit even if fully mobilized. IEA forecasts 1.5M bbl/d of Q2 demand destruction as the market's self-correction mechanism.

2. Demand destruction is bifurcated

  • Asia — price-led: Singapore premiums forcing rationing, airline cancellations accelerating, 2 of 3 Singapore refineries on bitumen force majeure.
  • Europe — policy-led: Slovenia imposed 50L/day fuel purchase limit. Italian airports restricting operations. EU activated 90-day strategic reserves. Grangemouth + Prax Lindsey closures reducing domestic refining capacity.
  • US — structural safe haven: Refinery runs +900K bbl/d YoY, stocks building. Domestic production insulates from worst of physical tightness.

3. Forward curve complacency

Dated Brent at $144/bbl vs futures at $95 — a $49 physical-paper disconnect at historic extremes. Physical WTI trading at a +$20 premium to financial. HFI Research (Apr 14) calls this "the biggest disconnect I've ever seen in the oil market." Goldman notes the 1.4B bbl cushion estimated at start of 2026 is now "a mirage." 230 loaded tankers stranded in the Persian Gulf represent both trapped supply and a ceasefire tail risk.

4. Freight is the binding constraint

NWE MR vessel availability at 6 ships (7-day window) vs 90-day average of 15 — a 60% collapse. TC2 at WS 312. Marine insurance premiums at 10x pre-war levels. Over 34,000 ships have diverted from the Strait. Even with arbitrage economics open, physical vessels cannot move the diesel.

5. Refinery cycling constraint

June Goh: refinery startup takes 4–7 days, shutdown only 1–2 days. Asian refiners cannot restart capacity even if Hormuz reopens partially — the asymmetric restart timeline means production recovery lags any political resolution by weeks. 2 of 3 Singapore refineries already on bitumen force majeure.

6. Fuel oil to gasoil feedback loop

Full chain: Hormuz blocks AG crude → lower VGO/straight-run supply → VGO diverted to hydrocrackers (maximize diesel) → straight-run to distillate units → less fuel oil blending feedstock → LSFO cracks rally → refiners blend gasoil DOWN into fuel oil to capture spread → gasoil supply tightens further → self-reinforcing loop.

Q3 LSFO 0.5%/Brent at $25–30/bbl vs historical mean $6–8. Hoa Nguyen (Apr 15): hedging flow caused pullback from $20 to $11–12 on prompt, but "this is not the kind of conflict that will conclude anytime soon" — expects rebound to $18–20/bbl for June/July.
